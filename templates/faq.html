Should I get the vaccination even if I already had COVID-19?

According to the University of Chicago's Medical School, "yes, absolutely. The SARS-CoV-2 virus is still circulating in our communities, and we haven’t reached herd-immunity levels of vaccination yet."

Are the side effects unbearable?

Pain is subjective and your mileage may vary on side effects. Side effects are natural and is your body's response to building immunity. Temporary pain is worth it if even one person's life is saved. More information.

Do I need to still wear a mask after getting vaccinated?

CDC states that "fully vaccinated people can resume activities without wearing a mask or physically distancing, except where required by federal, state, local, tribal, or territorial laws, rules, and regulations, including local business and workplace guidance."

When should I schedule my second shot?

. Pfizer requires a second shot 21 days after the first while Moderna requires a second shot 28 days after the initial dose and Johnson & Johnson is a single shot vaccine.

When is herd immunity achieved?

. Herd immunity for COVID-19 is achieved when about 70% of the population has been vaccinated or recovered.

How long does the vaccine last?

. Reports show that the vaccines last for six months, potentially longer.

Can my child get vaccinated?

As of June 2021, children age 12 and over are eligible to receive the FDA approved Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine.
